# Executive Summary
## Atlas Lab HR Analytics — Employee Attrition Analysis
**Prepared for:** Atlas Lab HR Leadership
**Period Analized:** Full employee lifecycle dataset (~1,200 employees)
**Overall atrition rate:** 16.12% · **Average Salary:** \$106,133

---

## Situation 

Atlas Lab was losing approximately 1 in 6 employees annually. At an average salary of \$106K, each departure carries an estimated replacement cost of ~\$53,000-\$85,000 (industry benchmark: 50-80% of annual salary), representing a potential annual exposure of \$8.13M if attrition continues unchecked.
The organization lacked visibulity into *who* was leaving and *why*, making retention efforts reactive and untargeted.

---

## Key Findings

**1. Overtime is the strongest attrition signal.**
Employees working overtime leave at nearly 3x the rate of those who don't.
This is a single highest-leverage variable in the dataset.

**2. The first year is the most critical window.**
Attrition peaks at year 0-1 of tenure. Employees who survive past year 2 show significantly higher retention. The onboarding experience is broken.

**3. Sales Representatives are in crisis.**
~40% attrition rate in this role — more than double the company average.
This is not a pipeline problem; it is a retention emergency in one function.

**4. Travel Frequency drives departure.**
Frequent traveleres leave 3x the rate of non-travelers. This is a structural policy issue, not an individual preference issue.

---

## Recomendations

| Priority | Action | Owner | Timeline |
| 🔴 High | Set overtime thresholds by role; enforce compensatory time-off policy | HR Ops + Line Managers | 30 days |
| 🔴 High | Redesign 90-day onboarding; add 6-month mandatory stay interview | HR Business Partners | 60 days |
| 🔴 High | Conduct sales compensation and career path review | HR + Sales Leadership | 45 days |
| 🟡 Medium | Audit travel requirements; introduce travel flexibility or rotation | HR + Operations | 90 days |
| 🟢 Ongoing | Conduct pay equity audit across ethnicity and gender | HR Analytics | Quarterly |

---

## Impact Estimate

Redicing attrition by **5 percentage points** (from 16.12% to -11%) would retain approximately 70 additional employees annually.

At a conservative replacement cost of \$53,000 per employee, this represents a potential saving of **\$7.7M per year** — achievable primarily through low-cost policy changes in overtime management and onboarding.
